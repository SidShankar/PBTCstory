EDUCATION
OUR STORY: PART THREE
Lebanon's public school system has not changed for many years (specifically from 1943 to present). Although many educators are extremely knowledgeable, they do not have the qualifications or the experience required in order to teach. As a result, Lebanese students are only able to get a sufficient education by attending private schools, a heavy financial burden on their families.
Ms. Ziadeh, the director of the Home of Tripoli for Social Welfare, has made large strides in order to compensate for this lack of education. Every one of her children is enrolled in a private school and receives tutoring.
